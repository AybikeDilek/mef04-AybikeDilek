Project Definition & Introduction

The term project aimed to conduct various exploratory analyses and to provide basic inferential analyses using House Sales in King County, USA dataset.

The main motivation of this project was exploring the feature of the sold houses in King Country, USA such as price, sqft_living, area and, creating a basic linear regression model to predict the price of the house base on its feature such as sqft_living.

About Dataset

The dataset contains the various data points for sold houses in King County, USA from 2014-05 to 2015-05, which are stored in a CSV file. There are 21 variables, with 21,613 observations. The variables were explained one by one.

  • id: Unique house id
  • date: Date of sale
  • price: Price of house
  • bedrooms: # of bedrooms
  • bathrooms: # of bathrooms/bedrooms
  • sqft_living: The square footage of the living area
  • sqft_lot: The square footage of the lot area
  • floors: Levels of the floor in a house
  • waterfront: A binary variable for whether the house has a water view or not
  • view: An rank from 0 to 4 to show the goodness of the view
  • condition: An rank from 1 to 5 about the condition of the house
  • grade: A rank from 1 to 13 about the construction and design
  • sqft_above: The square footage of the above basement area
  • sqft_basement: The square footage of the basement area
  • yr_built: Construction year
  • yr_renovated: Renovation year
  • zipcode: Zip code of the house
  • lat: Latitude of the house
  • long: Longitude of the house
  • sqft_living15: The square footage of the living area for the nearest 15 neighbors
  • sqft_lot15: The square footage of the lot area for the nearest 15 neighbors

Literature Review

During the literature review, it was seen that the same data set was used in the Assignment of Coursera IBM Data Science courses. In the assignment, prediction methods were mainly used. The output of the different models had been compared. Additionally, the multiple linear regression models were conducted and benchmarked base on R^2.

Besides, the R analysis of the same dataset was investigated. In this analysis, the statistical analysis was mainly used, such as Chi-Square and T-Test, and multiple linear regression models were conducted.

In this study, exploratory data analysis was mainly used and only simple linear regression models were conducted. Finally, the root mean squared error, the R^2 value and the residual distribution were used for comparison.

Overview

The packages were imported. The pandas and the numpy were used for data processing such as filtering, sorting, subsetting. The matplotlib and the seaborn were used for visualization. The folium was used for leaflet mapping. The datetime was used for preprocessing such as transforming the date_time column to the date_year_month column. The warnings was used for hiding warning messages. Lastly, sklearn.linear_model was used for the basic linear regression models.

Modelling

In this section, two basic linear regression models were created to predict the price of the houses.

The first model was run with sqft_living and price. As a result of the model, the Mean Squared Error and the R^2 value were calculated.

In [22]:
from sklearn.model_selection import train_test_split
from sklearn.linear_model import LinearRegression
x = data[["sqft_living"]].values
y = data["price"].values

X_train, X_test, y_train, y_test = train_test_split(x, y, test_size=0.2, random_state=0)
lm_1 = LinearRegression()
lm_1.fit(X_train, y_train)
y_pred = lm_1.predict(X_test)

from sklearn import metrics
print('Root Mean Squared Error:', np.sqrt(metrics.mean_squared_error(y_test, y_pred)).round(2))
print('R^2:',lm_1.score(X_train, y_train).round(3))

Root Mean Squared Error: 248879.06
R^2: 0.496
